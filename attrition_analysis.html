What is a Correlation Heatmap?

This heatmap shows the strength of correlation between numeric variables in the dataset, including Age, Tenure, Current Employee Rating.. and Is_Exited (attrition). Values range from −1 (strong negative correlation) to 1 (strong positive). Darker shades indicate stronger relationships. For example, there is a strong negative correlation between Tenure and Attrition (−0.73), suggesting that employees with lower tenure are more likely to leave. Other variables show very weak or negligible correlation with attrition, indicating limited predictive relevance.

What is a Chi-Square Heatmap?

The chart presents a Chi-Square test that examines whether there is a statistical dependency between categorical variables and employee attrition. Each row represents a feature, with the color indicating the strength of the association (Chi² value), and the p-value showing the statistical significance. The darker the color and the smaller the p-value (below 0.05), the more significant the relationship. The chart reveals that only the "DepartmentType" variable shows a significant association with attrition (p = 0.032), suggesting that the department type may influence employee turnover.

What is an ANOVA Test?

The graph presents the distribution of employee age by department type using a Box Plot, combined with an ANOVA test to examine whether there is a statistically significant difference in average age between departments. Each box represents the central age range within a department: the middle line shows the median age, the box edges represent the 25th and 75th percentiles (quartiles), and the "whiskers" indicate the youngest and oldest ages. Additional values on the sides (such as min, max, Q1, and Q3) help understand the spread of the data. The ANOVA test result at the top of the graph (F=0.26, p=0.93291) indicates that there is no significant difference in age between the departments (since the p-value is greater than 0.05), suggesting that age is likely not dependent on department.

ANOVA: Tenure vs Performance

The chart illustrates the relationship between employees' Performance Score and their Tenure using a Box Plot combined with an ANOVA test. Each box represents a group of employees based on their performance level ("Exceeds", "Fully Meets", "Needs Improvement", "PIP") and shows how tenure is distributed within each group. The middle line of the box indicates the median (the middle value), the box edges represent the first (Q1) and third (Q3) quartiles, and the whiskers show the minimum and maximum tenure values. Additional statistical values like mean, standard deviation, and other key figures are also annotated on the plot. The ANOVA test result, shown in the chart title (F=0.78, p=0.50496), evaluates whether there is a statistically significant difference in average tenure among the performance groups. Since the p-value is greater than 0.05, there is no statistical evidence to suggest a meaningful relationship between performance level and tenure in this dataset. To interpret whether there's a relationship between the variables, one should observe the shape and spread of the boxes: if there were noticeable differences in the medians or ranges (Q1–Q3) across the performance levels, and the p-value was less than 0.05, it would indicate a significant link between performance and tenure. However, in this case, the distributions are relatively similar, indicating no significant correlation between these two factors.
